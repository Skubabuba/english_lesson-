Epilogue - Justice and Reflection

The Final Chapter

He was extradited to Canada. The trial was intense. Disturbing footage was shown in court. His lawyers claimed he had severe mental illness.

But the jury didn't buy it.

Life in prison.

No chance of parole for 25 years. He now sits in a maximum-security facility in Quebec.

This story isn't just about a murderer. It's about how the internet created him, fed him, gave him power.

It's about how people who just wanted to protect kittens ended up tracking a killer.

And it's a warning: sometimes evil doesn't arrive with a scream. Sometimes it clicks "Upload," smiles behind a screen, and waits for the world to notice.
